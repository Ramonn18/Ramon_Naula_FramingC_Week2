Natural Disasters & Community Resilience in Athens, 2020–2026

The Risk Perception–Action Gap

Athens residents are acutely aware of disaster threats. Yet this awareness rarely translates into personal preparedness. The barrier is not knowledge — it is the perceived absence of individual efficacy and institutional support.

As disasters intensify and government coordination falters, community trust erodes. The result: a city suspended between awareness and action, where "cynical lethargy" fills the void left by failing infrastructure.

I have the knowledge of how to act during a disaster but I lack the individual efficacy and government support to take proper action.

— The Athenian Paradox
